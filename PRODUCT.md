# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Restaurant owners and managers in Bengaluru seeking verified listings of hospitality staff and vendors. They are busy operators — they land on the marketplace with a specific need (find a chef, find a linen supplier, check a restaurant's contact) and want to act fast. Secondary users are hospitality job seekers and B2B vendors browsing for leads.

## Product Purpose

Margros Marketplace is Bengaluru's verified directory for the hospitality industry — restaurants, staff, and vendors in one place. Success means a restaurant owner can discover a verified listing and make contact within two minutes. The "verified" badge is the core trust signal; the product's job is to surface it prominently and protect it from dilution.

## Brand Personality

Connected · Warm · Bengaluru-first. The product feels like a well-run city guide built by someone who knows the hospitality industry from the inside — not a generic SaaS tool. Warm but credible. Local but professional.

## Anti-references

- Swiggy / Zomato: consumer food-delivery aesthetic, too casual, bright oranges and consumer-grade UI
- Generic SaaS dashboards: navy + white, metric cards, impersonal
- JustDial / Sulekha: cluttered ad-heavy directories, low trust signal, visual noise

## Design Principles

1. **Trust signals first.** Verified status, ratings, and local identity must be immediately legible. Never let decorative UI compete with these.
2. **Reveal, don't overwhelm.** Contact details are gated behind auth — the reveal interaction must feel intentional and worth the sign-in. Don't apologize for the gate; frame it as exclusivity.
3. **Local identity is earned.** Bengaluru is part of the product, not a tag. Area names, local dining categories, and familiar neighborhood context should read naturally.
4. **Density serves the task.** Restaurant owners scan lists fast. Compact cards with the right hierarchy beat airy designs that waste vertical space.
5. **Consistency over surprise.** Same button shape, same chip vocabulary, same reveal pattern across restaurants, staff, and vendors.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. All interactive elements keyboard-accessible. Contact reveal must work without hover (touch-friendly tap to reveal). Reduced motion: skip blur animations, use instant opacity switch instead.
